Track My Health

Introduction

The Centralized Health Record (CHR) system is a digital version of a patient's paper records, a platform where a patient's medical information such as medical history, diagnoses, medications, treatment plans, allergies, radiology images, and laboratory test results, and other relevant health information is stored in a digital format and can be accessed by authorized healthcare providers across different hospitals or healthcare facilities. This type of system can offer several benefits, such as improved coordination of care, reduced duplication of tests, and better-informed medical decisions

Benefits

As technology continues to advance, The adoption of CHR systems will be very useful in healthcare organizations as they offer numerous advantages in terms of patient care, data management, operational efficiency, better patient engagement, and the ability to generate reports and analytics for monitoring and improving healthcare outcomes. However, the implementation and use of CHR systems also require careful consideration of factors such as data security, user training, and adherence to healthcare regulations and Privacy laws

Features

Centralised Health Record system provides a centralised information of patient records, accessibility for authorised health care professionals, interoperability within multiple hospitals or healthcare entities, patient portals that empower individuals to access their health information by their own, and also enhances data security & privacy of the user and as well as the hospital. These systems contribute to improved care coordination and clinical decision support

Security

Implementing a centralized CHR system requires careful planning, collaboration with healthcare professionals, & should complince with relevant health care regulations & privacy laws and a strong focus on security and privacy to build trust among patients and healthcare providers.
